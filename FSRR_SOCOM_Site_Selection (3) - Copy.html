The satellite training site distributes facilities across the 4IMB's pre-designated zones to separate garrison from training, maximize range safety, and enable concurrent multi-phase operations. The cantonment (Zone 5) anchors the complex at the geographic center, with specialized training radiating outward.

Section 03 — Scout Sniper
Scout Sniper Training Facilities

Section 05 — Joint Force
Army SOF — Joint Force Integration

Designed from the ground up for joint operations. The JSOC-Forward co-locates Army SOF with PAF, PN, and allied liaison elements. Zone 9 provides dedicated joint exercise space for JTAC certification, fire support coordination, and combined SOF-conventional maneuver. Zone 8's 4,584-hectare live fire sector enables battalion-scale combined arms integrating air, ground, and maritime components.

Section 09
Site Selection Summary

The 45-facility layout transforms Camp Kibaritan's Zone 5 into a purpose-built satellite training site for three mission-essential pillars: scout sniper training with progressive ranges from 600m through 1,500m, special reconnaissance with full-mission-profile capability from insertion to extraction, and Army SOF integration into the Joint Force through a co-located operations center and dedicated combined exercise areas. Every facility is geo-located within pre-designated zones — requiring no reclassification — ready for development upon approval.
